Our Standard

The record we keep, and the statutes it answers to.

Boatcheckin is built around the documentation charter operators are expected to produce under US federal maritime rules, Florida state regulations, and electronic signature law. This page maps each statute to the records the software helps you capture.

Read this first

References are descriptive. Not interpretive. Not legal advice.

The statutes on this page are cited because they describe the records an operator is typically expected to keep — not because Boatcheckin attests that any individual operator is compliant. Compliance belongs to the operator.

Each section pairs a plain-English summary of what a statute requires with a description of how Boatcheckin supports the corresponding recordkeeping. For advice specific to your operation, consult a licensed attorney.

Statutes are subject to amendment. The original text on the issuing body's website is always authoritative. If we appear to describe a statute incorrectly, email hello@boatcheckin.com and we'll correct this page.

US Federal Maritime Standards

United States Coast Guard & 46 CFR.

The federal baseline for small passenger vessel operations — including safety instruction and manifest expectations for most inspected charters.

Federal · 46 CFR
Pre-Departure Safety Orientation
46 CFR §185.506

Small passenger vessel operators must provide each passenger with a safety orientation before getting underway — covering life preserver stowage, emergency exits, fire extinguishers, distress signals, and emergency actions.

The master is responsible for ensuring this instruction happens on every voyage. Historically proven by verbal briefings — effective in the moment, but difficult to document after the fact.

How Boatcheckin supports the record
Each guest acknowledges a per-topic safety card on their own device — life preserver, emergency exits, fire extinguisher, distress signals, emergency actions. Each acknowledgment is timestamped with delivery method, IP, and user agent. The captain attestation seals the set. The record shows who was told what, when, and how they confirmed.
Federal · USCG
Passenger Manifest Requirements
46 CFR Subchapter T · USCG guidance

Small passenger vessels carrying six or more passengers are typically expected to maintain a passenger list — name, date, voyage identifier — for every trip. The operator must be able to produce, on request, a record of who was aboard on a given voyage.

How Boatcheckin supports the record
Every trip generates a USCG-format manifest PDF on demand — vessel, voyage ID, departure time, captain of record, and every guest. Available for export without a support ticket. Produce it in thirty seconds, not thirty minutes.
Federal · OUPV/Master
Master's Authority & Responsibility
46 U.S.C. §8104 · USCG licensing

The licensed captain holds ultimate authority and responsibility for the vessel, passengers, crew, and operation. No software displaces that authority.

How Boatcheckin supports the record
The captain's decision to start the trip is an explicit, attested action — a slide-to-start confirmation, cryptographically recorded with timestamp. The software never auto-starts a trip. The captain directs. Boatcheckin records.
Florida State Standards

Florida Statutes & FWC Chapter 327.

Florida applies state-specific requirements on top of federal maritime rules, administered primarily by FWC.

Florida · FWC
Vessel Safety & Boating Safety ID
Florida Statutes Chapter 327

Chapter 327 covers registration, safety equipment, operator conduct, and the Boating Safety ID Card requirement for operators of vessels of 10+ horsepower. Guests who take the helm of a bareboat charter are often subject to this requirement.

How Boatcheckin supports the record
The trip link prompts for Boating Safety ID details where applicable, captures a record of the check, and ties it to the guest profile and trip manifest. No surprises at the dock.
Florida · Livery
Livery & Rental Vessel Requirements
F.S. §327.54

Livery operators must provide renters with pre-operation instruction including vessel operation, safety equipment, local hazards, and applicable state laws, plus recordkeeping on renter identity and certifications.

How Boatcheckin supports the record
Livery operators can structure the guest flow to include renter ID, equipment-review acknowledgments, operation instructions, and local-hazard briefings — each as discrete attested items. The paper trail the statute describes, kept without the paper.
Florida · Insurance
Insurance Referral Structure
F.S. §626.112 et seq.

Florida restricts who may sell, solicit, or negotiate insurance. Referral arrangements are permitted only under specific conditions.

How Boatcheckin structures referrals
Boatcheckin does not sell, solicit, negotiate, bind, or service insurance. Where referenced, insurance is offered by a licensed agent. Boatcheckin receives a fixed referral fee regardless of policy purchase. The customer's relationship is with the licensed agent — not us.
Electronic Signature Law

ESIGN & UETA — when a digital signature holds up in court.

Electronic waivers and attestations are legally equivalent to ink signatures when the record establishes identity, intent, consent to electronic form, and document integrity.

Federal · ESIGN
Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act
15 U.S.C. §7001 et seq.

ESIGN establishes that electronic signatures have the same legal validity as ink signatures — provided the signer consented to electronic form, identity can be attributed, and the record is accurately retained.

How Boatcheckin supports the four ESIGN tests
Intent. Affirmative signature act tied to specific waiver text. Consent to electronic form. Shown at the top of the flow. Attribution. Timestamp, IP, user agent, verified guest identity. Record integrity. SHA-256 hashed at signing — any modification detectable.
Florida · UETA
Uniform Electronic Transaction Act
F.S. Chapter 668, Part I (§668.50)

Florida's UETA provides the same foundational rule as ESIGN at state level — an electronic record cannot be denied legal effect solely because it is electronic.

How Boatcheckin supports UETA alignment
Electronic-records consent is captured explicitly. Retention follows statutory windows. Records are stored in a retrievable, accurately reproducible form throughout retention. The record is admissible because the record is intact.
Privacy & Consent Standards

The rules that follow the guest's data, not the vessel.

Privacy law applies at the layer of each individual guest — not the operator's state.

Federal · TCPA
Telephone Consumer Protection Act
47 U.S.C. §227

TCPA governs commercial SMS and automated voice messages. Express written consent is required for most marketing messages.

How Boatcheckin supports compliance
SMS is used for operational trip messages tied to confirmed bookings only. Marketing SMS requires explicit opt-in. Operator messages to guests without a booking are not supported.
Federal · CAN-SPAM
CAN-SPAM Act
15 U.S.C. §7701 et seq.

CAN-SPAM sets federal standards for commercial email — sender identification, accurate subject lines, physical address, and functional unsubscribe.

How Boatcheckin supports compliance
Every operator email carries sender identity, postal address, and unsubscribe link. Marketing guest data is cleared on a 90-day cycle unless opted in. Unsubscribe propagates immediately across channels.
State & International
CCPA, GDPR & Guest Data Rights
Cal. Civ. Code §1798.100 · EU GDPR

California guests enjoy CCPA rights (access, deletion, opt-out). EEA/UK guests enjoy broader GDPR rights. These apply per-guest even when the vessel never leaves Florida waters.

How Boatcheckin supports compliance
Guests can request access, export, or deletion through the trip link or directly. Compliance records — signed waivers, safety attestations, manifests — are retained for statutory periods and may be exempt from deletion. The trip record stays intact; the marketing layer does not.
Honesty about scope

What Boatcheckin does not claim.

A standards page without this section is overpromising.

We don't certify your compliance.

Using Boatcheckin does not make an operator compliant. We support the documentation; we do not attest to it on your behalf.

We don't interpret the law.

Every statute has edge cases and jurisdictional variations. Your attorney can address those; we cannot.

We don't draft your waivers.

Waiver language is specific to your operation. We capture and hash the text you provide — we don't generate or review it.

We don't replace the captain.

No record replaces the go/no-go call. No checklist certifies sea conditions. We document what the captain decided.
