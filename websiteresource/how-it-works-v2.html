Part 01 — Setup

Fifteen minutes, and you never do it again.

Configure your vessel details, waiver language, and safety briefing once. Every future trip pulls from those defaults.

1
~4 minutes

Add your vessel

Boat Wizard captures vessel name, registration, hailing port, passenger capacity, and operating area. For multi-vessel fleets, add each boat once.

2
~4 minutes

Your waiver, word for word

Paste in the waiver text your operation already uses — the one your insurer reviewed. We don't rewrite it. We capture it precisely.

3
~4 minutes

Safety briefing cards

PFD location, fire extinguisher, man-overboard procedure — each goes in as a card. Guests see and acknowledge each one individually.

4
~3 minutes

Compliance settings

Set your operating jurisdiction, retention window, and electronic-signature consent language. The defaults are conservative.

Outcome

Your account is ready. The next trip you create is live in thirty seconds.

Part 02 — Every trip

Create the trip. Send the link. That's it.

Every trip starts the same way — a few fields, one generated link, one message sent to the booker.

1
~20 seconds

Create the trip

Enter the date, select the vessel, set expected guest count. One cryptographically signed link is generated.

2
~10 seconds

Send to the booker

One message, one link. The booker shares it with the rest of the party. You do not collect guest phone numbers. You do not chase.

3
Happens on its own

Watch the manifest fill

As guests complete registration, the manifest populates in real time. You see who's signed and who's outstanding.

Outcome

Your 10am charter is already live before 8am. Operator time: under 60 seconds.

Part 03 — The guest

No app. No account. No captain chasing signatures.

A paying guest on a Saturday morning should not be installing an app to sign a waiver.

1
Any phone, any language

Tap the link

Opens in any browser. No download, no sign-up. Localized automatically to the guest's phone language.

2
~60 seconds

Identify and verify

Name, email, phone. A one-time code confirms identity. The data goes to the operator, not to advertisers.

3
~60 seconds

Read and sign the waiver

The guest sees your waiver in full. They draw or type their signature. Text, signature, timestamp, IP, and cryptographic hash are captured together.

4
~30 seconds

Acknowledge each safety card

One card at a time. Each tap is attributed. No "I've read the terms" blanket box pretending to be a briefing.

5
Instant

Done — confirmation sent

The guest gets a clean confirmation. The operator's manifest updates. Nobody typed anything twice.

Outcome

Every guest is verified, signed, and briefed — before they arrive. Guest time: ~3 minutes total.

Part 04 — The captain

The cleanest pre-departure view of your life.

One URL, one glance, one decision. No login — captains shouldn't type passwords on a pier with salt spray.

Manifest at a glance

Every guest registered for this trip, with status flags. Green if signed. Red if missing.

Safety card status

Per-guest acknowledgment status. A missing card is visible immediately.

Emergency contacts

Every guest's emergency contact, one tap to dial. Plus USCG Ch. 16 and FWC hotline.

Slide to start

A deliberate, attestable start gate — timestamped, cryptographically recorded, and sealed.

Outcome

The captain's go/no-go decision is based on what's actually true right now. Captain time: under 60 seconds.

What you walk away with

A sealed record — intact, attributable, available.

Two years from now, when an insurer asks or a regulator audits, this is what's there to answer.

01

Hashed with SHA-256

Waiver text, signature, timestamp, and IP are combined and hashed at signing. Any modification is detectable.

02

Every action has an actor

Creation, signing, captain start, trip completion — each produces an audit entry with who, when, and how.

03

Trip is locked at completion

The audit trail is sealed. No row can be silently altered. Corrections require a new, attributed entry.

04

5 years minimum retention

Default five years — longer where your jurisdiction requires. You control extension; we never shorten.

05

Exportable, any time

Complete PDF package — waivers, acknowledgments, manifest, audit trail. One attachment to your insurer.

06

Encrypted at rest and in transit

TLS 1.3 over the wire, AES-256-class encryption at rest, row-level isolation at the database tier.
